# Tenant — Jobs to Be Done

> Inferred emotional and social dimensions are marked with *(validate)* — the designer should confirm these with real users.

---

## Primary job

**"When I need a place to live or work in a new area, I want to find and secure a rental that fits my needs, so I can settle in and get on with my life."**

### Functional dimension
Find available properties, evaluate them against personal criteria (location, size, price, features), visit the best options, get accepted as a tenant, and formalize the rental agreement.

### Emotional dimension *(validate)*
Feel in control of a high-stakes decision. Renting is one of the largest recurring expenses — the process should feel transparent, not opaque. Reduce the anxiety of committing to a place without enough information.

### Social dimension *(validate)*
Be perceived as a responsible, reliable candidate by the property manager. The tenant's reputation directly affects whether they get accepted — they want to present themselves as trustworthy and financially stable.

## Secondary jobs

### Prove financial reliability before visiting

**"When I want to visit a property I'm interested in, I want to demonstrate that I'm financially trustworthy, so the manager takes my visit request seriously."**

- **Functional:** Provide documentation (credit report, income proof) that establishes financial credibility. Have this ready before the first interaction, not scrambled together after.
- **Emotional** *(validate)*: Feel prepared and confident going into the process, not caught off guard by unexpected requirements.
- **Social** *(validate)*: Be seen as someone who has their affairs in order — not someone the manager has to chase for documents.

### Narrow down options without wasting time

**"When I'm browsing many available properties, I want to quickly filter out the ones that don't match what I need, so I only spend time on places that could actually work."**

- **Functional:** Filter by location/zone, room count, surface area, price range, building features (elevator, garage, pool), pet policy, and utility services.
- **Emotional** *(validate)*: Avoid the overwhelm of scrolling through dozens of irrelevant listings. Feel like the search is getting somewhere.
- **Social** *(validate)*: Minimal — this is a private activity.

### Evaluate a property thoroughly before committing to a visit

**"When I find a property that looks promising, I want to understand its full details — rooms, layout, location, amenities — so I can decide if it's worth visiting in person."**

- **Functional:** See the property's exact address, room types with dimensions, a visual layout/floor plan, a photo gallery, the intended use (residential/commercial), and the monthly price.
- **Emotional** *(validate)*: Feel informed enough to make a confident decision about whether to invest time in a visit. Avoid the disappointment of visiting a place that doesn't match expectations.
- **Social** *(validate)*: Minimal — this is a private activity.

### Schedule a visit that fits my calendar

**"When I decide to visit a property, I want to pick a date and time that works for me from available options, so I can plan my day around it."**

- **Functional:** See which dates have open visit slots, choose a specific time, and get confirmation that the slot is reserved for me. Receive a calendar event so I don't forget.
- **Emotional** *(validate)*: Feel like the process respects my time — I'm choosing, not being told when to show up. The confirmation (email with calendar attachment) provides reassurance that the visit is real and tracked.
- **Social** *(validate)*: Minimal — though showing up prepared and on time to the visit matters for the impression on the manager.

### Understand the rental agreement before signing

**"When I'm offered a contract, I want to understand every term — rent, price adjustments, warranty requirements, penalties — so I don't commit to something I'll regret."**

- **Functional:** Review the contract details: pricing periods and how rent changes over time, escalation rules (which rate index, how often), fines for late payment, early termination conditions, warranty/guarantee requirements.
- **Emotional** *(validate)*: Feel protected, not trapped. Argentine rental contracts are multi-year commitments with legally mandated price adjustments — understanding the escalation formula is the difference between confidence and anxiety.
- **Social** *(validate)*: Be perceived by landlord and manager as an engaged, serious tenant who reads the contract — not someone who signs blindly.

### Formalize the agreement with legal validity

**"When both parties agree to the rental terms, I want to sign the contract in a way that's legally binding, so neither side can dispute the agreement later."**

- **Functional:** Complete a digital signature process that produces a legally recognized document. Have the signed PDF stored and accessible.
- **Emotional** *(validate)*: Feel that the agreement is final and secure. The digital process should feel as legitimate as signing physical papers — not like clicking "I agree" on a website.
- **Social** *(validate)*: Be seen as someone who follows through on commitments. The act of signing is a mutual trust signal.

### Track and prove my payments

**"When I pay rent or utilities each month, I want to record that payment with proof, so there's no dispute about whether I paid."**

- **Functional:** Upload a receipt for each payment — rent, municipal fees (ABL), water, electricity, gas — and have it stored alongside the contract. See recent receipts to confirm what's been submitted.
- **Emotional** *(validate)*: Feel protected against the "he said, she said" of payment disputes. Have peace of mind that there's an auditable trail.
- **Social** *(validate)*: Be perceived as a responsible tenant who pays on time and keeps records — this reputation matters for future rentals.

### Learn how the platform works when I get stuck

**"When I encounter a requirement I don't understand — like what a credit report is or how to format my phone number — I want to find a clear explanation, so I can unblock myself without asking someone."**

- **Functional:** Access help articles that explain specific requirements: what documents are needed, phone number format (Argentine E.164), what a credit report is and how to get one.
- **Emotional** *(validate)*: Feel capable, not stupid. The requirement should feel achievable, not like an arbitrary gatekeeping hurdle.
- **Social** *(validate)*: Minimal — this is private self-service.

## Consumption jobs

These are frictions in using the platform itself — valuable for the designer to address:

- **Onboarding:** Choose the right account type (tenant) and understand what profile information is needed upfront, before hitting a gate later.
- **Profile completion:** Encrypt-and-store personal data (name, DNI, CUIL, phone) — the tenant needs to trust that sharing sensitive information is safe.
- **Document upload:** Understand which document types are needed (credit report is mandatory for booking) and have them digitized before starting the property search.
- **Digital signature onboarding:** If the tenant doesn't have a digital certificate, they need to complete an identity verification process with an external provider before they can sign. This is a significant friction point — it's outside the platform's control but blocks a critical step.
- **Receipt management:** Remember to upload receipts monthly. The platform shows the last 2 months — if the tenant forgets, there's no retroactive reminder.
